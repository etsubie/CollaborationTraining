Steve Jobs

An American man who was the co-founder of Apple Inc. and played a major role in revolutionazing the technology industry

Here are some key dates and events from Steve Jobs' life:

  • 1955 : Steve Jobs is born in San Francisco, California.
  • 1969 : Jobs graduates from high school and enrolls in Reed College in Portland, Oregon. He drops out after one semester but continues to audit classes for the next 18 months.
  • 1974 : Jobs returns to California and begins attending meetings of the Homebrew Computer Club, a group of computer hobbyists.
  • 1976 : Jobs co-founds Apple Computer with Steve Wozniak in his parents' garage. The company's first product is the Apple I computer.
  • 1977 : Apple introduces the Apple II, the first personal computer to have color graphics.
  • 1980 : Apple goes public, making Jobs a millionaire at age 25.
  • 1983 : Apple introduces the Lisa computer but it is not a commercial success.
  • 1984 : Apple introduces the Macintosh computer, which becomes an instant success.
  • 1985 : Jobs resigns from Apple after a power struggle with the company's board of directors.
  • 1986 : Jobs founds NeXT Computer, a company that makes high-end computers for the education and business markets.
  • 1986 : Jobs buys Pixar Animation Studios from George Lucas for $10 million.
  • 1991 : Apple buys NeXT Computer for $400 million and brings Jobs back to the company as an advisor.
  • 1997: Jobs is named interim CEO of Apple, which is struggling financially and losing market share to Microsoft and other rivals.
  • 1998 : Apple introduces the iMac, a consumer-oriented desktop computer that quickly becomes a hit with consumers and helps turn around the company's fortunes.
  • 2001 : Apple introduces the iPod, a portable music player that becomes a cultural phenomenon and helps transform the music industry.
  • 2003 : Jobs is diagnosed with pancreatic cancer and undergoes surgery to remove the tumor.
  • 2007 : Apple introduces the iPhone, a revolutionary smartphone that changes the way people interact with technology.
  • 2008 : Jobs steps down as CEO of Apple due to his declining health but remains with the company as chairman of the board.
  • 2011 : Steve Jobs passes away at the age of 56 due to complications from pancreatic cancer.
